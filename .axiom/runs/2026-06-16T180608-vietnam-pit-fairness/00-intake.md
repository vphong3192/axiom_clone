# Intake — Vietnam PIT Fairness vs. Other Countries and Foreigners

- **Lane:** Full process
- **Effort:** high-stakes
- **Deliverable:** A structured policy analysis (English, 2000–3000 words) examining whether Vietnam's personal income tax (PIT) is unfairly structured:
  - Current PIT law in Vietnam (rates, brackets, deductions, social contributions)
  - Comparable PIT systems in 4–6 peer countries (Thailand, Philippines, Indonesia, South Korea, or others TBD)
  - Treatment of foreign nationals vs. Vietnamese nationals under Vietnamese law
  - Definition and evaluation of "fairness" (horizontal equity, vertical equity, burden distribution)
  - Explicit confidence grades (A/B/C) on all factual claims
  - Clear statement of which assertions are proven, reasoned, or remain debated
- **Audience & register:** Policy researcher or educated general reader; neutral, analytical tone; not advocacy
- **Running every step now?** Yes. This requires fresh research into current Vietnamese tax law (2026), comparable country data, and careful policy analysis. No prior work is reusable here.
- **Open questions:**
  1. Who is the intended audience — academic, policy brief, personal research, or publication?
  2. Which countries should be included in the comparison? (Developed, regional, ASEAN peers, or broader?)
  3. Is the task asking us to *defend* the fairness claim, *critique* it, or *evaluate* it neutrally?
  4. What foreign-worker categories should be examined? (All foreigners, knowledge workers, manual labor?)
  5. Should analysis include enforcement disparities and non-compliance rates, or law on the books only?
  6. What time horizon — current law only, or historical context?

**Agreed scope (one paragraph):**
Conduct an evidence-based policy analysis of Vietnam's personal income tax system to evaluate claims of unfairness in two dimensions: (1) whether Vietnam's PIT design is structurally less fair than comparable countries by standard tax-policy metrics (horizontal and vertical equity, burden distribution, compliance), and (2) whether Vietnam's law creates unequal treatment of foreign nationals vs. Vietnamese nationals. The analysis will gather verified current law from Vietnamese tax authority sources, select comparable countries' PIT systems, define fairness metrics explicitly, compare using reasoned analysis, and clearly label confidence on all factual findings and normative judgments.
