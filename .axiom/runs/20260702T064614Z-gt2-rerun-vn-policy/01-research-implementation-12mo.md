## Key findings

### 1. The launch and its legal architecture (dates and document tiers, kept distinct)

- **[A]** Nghị quyết 60-NQ/TW — a **Party** document (Ban Chấp hành Trung ương Đảng khóa XIII, Hội nghị lần thứ 11), dated **12/4/2025** — set the political mandate/list for reorganizing provincial units into 34, ahead of any National Assembly action. Source: tulieuvankien.dangcongsan.vn full text (PDF), corroborated by luatvietnam.vn and congdankhuyenhoc.vn — https://tulieuvankien.dangcongsan.vn/Uploads/2025/4/7/13/NQ-60-TW.PDF ; https://luatvietnam.vn/chinh-sach/nghi-quyet-60-nq-tw-cua-ban-chap-hanh-trung-uong-ve-hoi-nghi-lan-thu-11-ban-chap-hanh-trung-uong-dang-khoa-xiii-397246-d1.html
- **[A]** Nghị quyết 202/2025/QH15 — a **National Assembly (Quốc hội, state legislature)** resolution — was **passed (thông qua) the morning of 12/6/2025**, with 96.44% of voting delegates in favor, reducing the country to 34 provincial-level units. The government portal's own account states the resolution **took legal effect from the same day it was passed, 12/6/2025** ("Nghị quyết có hiệu lực từ ngày 12/6"). Source: xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn full text, fetched directly — https://xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn/toan-van-nghi-quyet-so-202-2025-qh15-ve-sap-xep-don-vi-hanh-chinh-cap-tinh-119250612174148722.htm
- **[A]** Luật số 72/2025/QH15 (Tổ chức chính quyền địa phương, sửa đổi) — also a **Quốc hội** law, but a **separate document from Nghị quyết 202**, passed **16/6/2025** (Kỳ họp thứ 9, khóa XV), which set up the operating model (two-tier: cấp tỉnh + cấp xã) and the transition rules. Source: xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn full text — https://xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn/toan-van-luat-so-72-2025-qh15-to-chuc-chinh-quyen-dia-phuong-119250618161434371.htm
- **[A]** The **operational start** of the new two-tier apparatus (34 provinces, 3,321 communes, abolition of 696 district-level units) was **01/7/2025** — a date distinct from both the 12/6/2025 passage/effect date of Nghị quyết 202 and the 16/6/2025 passage of Luật 72. This is confirmed by multiple contemporaneous outlets (nhandan.vn, vtv.vn, baochinhphu.vn) published on/around 30/6–1/7/2025. Source: https://nhandan.vn/infographic-mo-hinh-to-chuc-chinh-quyen-dia-phuong-2-cap-van-hanh-tu-172025-post890580.html ; https://vtv.vn/infographic-bo-may-chinh-quyen-dia-phuong-2-cap-chinh-thuc-van-hanh-tu-1-7-2025-100250630215549817.htm
- **Note on precision:** these three dates/documents must NOT be collapsed. Party mandate (12/4/2025, Đảng) → NA resolution passed+effective (12/6/2025, Quốc hội) → NA organization law passed (16/6/2025, Quốc hội) → operational go-live (1/7/2025). All four are distinct and each has its own document tier.

### 2. Implementation problems reported since 1/7/2025

- **[A]** Commune-level overload is real and documented with a concrete case: in Hoài Đức commune (Hà Nội), as of the article's publication **10/6/2026**, ~70 staff handle work equivalent to that of 10 former district-level departments, process roughly 15,000 dossiers over the reviewed period (~1,500/month), covering 924 assigned tasks and 430 procedures. Party Secretary Nguyễn Thanh Bình and other officials are quoted describing overwork and officials handling unfamiliar specializations (land, construction, urban planning) with "fear of errors deterring decision-making." Source: dantri.com.vn, fetched — https://dantri.com.vn/noi-vu/xa-70-can-bo-ganh-viec-10-phong-cua-huyen-cu-xu-ly-1500-ho-sothang-20260609212731740.htm
- **[B]** IT/data-system fragmentation: commune-level digital-transformation staffing is reported short by roughly 40%, and officials must operate multiple sector-specific software systems (health, education, natural resources) that are not yet interoperable, forcing dual paper+digital reporting. This is a WebSearch-aggregated synthesis of several outlets (fit.uit.edu.vn, mst.gov.vn, vnpt.vn) that I did not individually fetch in full — capped at B, needs cross-check for the specific 40% figure. Source (search only): https://fit.uit.edu.vn/index.php/tin-tuc/tin-moi/7194-cap-xa-thieu-nhan-luc-cong-nghe-thong-tin-cho-chuyen-doi-so ; https://mst.gov.vn/dich-vu-cong-truc-tuyen-sau-sap-nhap-khac-phuc-han-che-ve-ha-tang-nhan-luc-quy-trinh-197251019082117052.htm
- **[C]** A commonly cited figure that communes now absorb "1,069 tasks formerly at district level and 106 formerly at provincial level" appears in aggregated search summaries but **could not be independently located** in the specific article I fetched (vietnamnet.vn, 27/6/2026, which discusses the qualitative overload problem but does not cite these numbers). Downgraded to C / flagged as unverified — do not present as a solid number.
- **[A]** Paperwork/ID-document transition: citizens are **not required** to renew ID cards, household-registration records, or other documents whose validity has not expired merely because of the locality-name change; renewal, when done, is **free of charge** (fee waiver) and can be done via VNeID/national public-service portal. This is grounded in Nghị quyết 190 (transitional provisions referenced in the law-explainer press) and confirmed by a Tuổi Trẻ report from 17/4/2025 on the CCCD fee waiver. Source: https://tuoitre.vn/mien-phi-cap-doi-can-cuoc-cong-dan-khi-sap-nhap-dia-gioi-hanh-chinh-2025041712285359.htm (fetched via search synthesis, cross-checked against multiple law-portal explainers — luatvietnam.vn, thuvienphapluat.vn)
- **[A]** Surplus public offices (trụ sở dôi dư): the Ministry of Home Affairs (Bộ Nội vụ) compiled a figure of **38,182 provincial-level public offices** nationwide (across the 52 provinces/cities being reorganized), of which **33,956** would continue in use and **4,226** were identified as surplus, as of the report published **10/5/2025** (i.e., before the 1/7/2025 go-live — a pre-transition estimate, not a post-transition count). Source: dantri.com.vn, fetched — https://dantri.com.vn/noi-vu/4226-tru-so-cong-doi-du-sau-sap-nhap-tinh-xu-ly-sao-20250510090623936.htm
- **[B]** Ho Chi Minh City, as one example of a locality actually implementing disposal of surplus offices, was instructed by the Ministry of Finance to complete final asset-handling plans **before 30/6/2026** and finish all related legal paperwork **before 31/12/2026** — this is a live, in-window administrative deadline (not yet elapsed at strand cutoff). Source (WebSearch synthesis of vneconomy.vn / tapchikinhtetaichinh.vn, not individually fetched in full) — cap at B.
- **[B]** Cadre relocation/housing support: several provinces enacted local council resolutions to subsidize officials who must relocate to the new provincial capital — e.g., An Giang: ~2,000,000 VND/month transport + 1,500,000 VND/month activity allowance + 1,500,000 VND/month lodging; Tuyên Quang cadres (relocating within the new Tuyên Quang/Hà Giang merged province) received 2,000,000 VND/month lodging + 1,000,000 VND/month transport. These are WebSearch-aggregated figures from local-government news sites I did not individually fetch — capped at B, treat exact VND figures as needing a follow-up primary-document check if used precisely in the deliverable.

### 3. Official one-year assessment process and its own timeline (all dates precise)

- **[A]** Phó Thủ tướng Phạm Thị Thanh Trà signed **Quyết định số 706/QĐ-TTg, ngày 21/4/2026** (a **Government/executive**-tier document, not a Party or NA document), launching the official plan for a nationwide one-year review ("sơ kết") of the administrative reorganization and two-tier government model. The plan's own internal deadlines: ministries/agencies report to the Ministry of Home Affairs by **10/5/2026**; provinces complete their own xã→tỉnh review and report by **15/5/2026**; the Central Steering Committee (Ban Chỉ đạo Trung ương) holds its own review before **20/6/2026**. Source: baochinhphu.vn, fetched — https://baochinhphu.vn/so-ket-1-nam-sap-xep-don-vi-hanh-chinh-van-hanh-chinh-quyen-dia-phuong-2-cap-102260422153251495.htm
- **[A]** A government progress report published **2/1/2026** (covering roughly 1/7/2025–31/12/2025, i.e. the first ~6 months) states: over 3,300 administrative procedures integrated into the national public-service portal; 3,139 commune-level public administrative service centers operating nationwide; ~90% of locally-handled dossiers processed online in that period; the share of procedures under local (as opposed to central) jurisdiction rose to 56% (from ~33% before); nearly 4,000 cadres were redeployed to commune level; the article explicitly states there was "zero disruption" to service continuity during transition but also states, without giving specifics, that "significant difficulties and challenges remain." Source: baochinhphu.vn, fetched — https://baochinhphu.vn/cach-mang-tinh-gon-bo-may-van-hanh-chinh-quyen-dia-phuong-hai-cap-dau-an-cai-cach-lich-su-102251230125915563.htm (published 2/1/2026 per byline, despite URL date-string reading 251230 which is the CMS's internal id, not necessarily the display date — treat display date 2/1/2026 as primary since that's what the fetch reported)
- **[A]** The formal one-year anniversary event took place **01/7/2026** (exactly at the strand's own cutoff date). Two related but distinguishable things happened that day, per government/state-media coverage I fetched directly:
  1. A conference "reviewing one year of implementing the two-tier local government model," at which **Deputy PM Phạm Thị Thanh Trà** (Government tier) reported: 705 commune-level units still do not meet the merger standards and will be studied for further consolidation "to be completed definitively in 2026"; roughly 1,300 implementing legal documents issued; a nearly 40% reduction in administrative procedures/business conditions; 27.4% of procedures remain at central-ministry level; 1,382 tasks decentralized to provincial authorities and 938 to communes. Named provinces cited for good implementation: Hà Nội, TP.HCM, Hải Phòng, Đồng Nai, Bắc Ninh, Quảng Ninh, Ninh Bình, Tây Ninh. Source: dantri.com.vn, fetched — https://dantri.com.vn/noi-vu/nghien-cuu-tiep-tuc-sap-nhap-xa-hoan-thanh-dut-diem-trong-2026-20260701101830685.htm
  2. Separately, state media (VTV) covered remarks framed around **General Secretary Tô Lâm** (Party tier) at what VTV describes as the same 1/7/2026 one-year milestone: provinces reduced 63→34, communes reduced 10,035→3,321, government workforce down "over 247,000 positions since 2021" (this is a **cumulative figure since 2021**, not attributable solely to the 2025 merger — should not be presented as a merger-specific number), over 2,000 procedures/business conditions simplified. Tô Lâm is quoted (from an earlier date, 31/10/2024, reused in this retrospective) saying central-level streamlining must precede provincial streamlining. VTV's own article also acknowledges past mechanical/uneven implementation as a lesson learned. Source: vtv.vn, fetched — https://vtv.vn/mot-nam-sap-xep-lai-giang-son-tinh-gon-gan-dan-hieu-qua-100260701224404466.htm
  - **Conflict/gap flag:** one WebSearch-engine-generated summary (not a page I opened) described this July 1 event using the phrase "mô hình chính quyền 3 cấp" (three-tier), which contradicts every primary source I fetched (all say "2 cấp," two-tier — provincial + commune, no intermediate district tier). I treat the "3 cấp" phrasing as a search-aggregator paraphrase error, not a fact, and rely on the directly-fetched full texts, which are unanimous on "chính quyền địa phương 2 cấp."
  - It is not fully clear from what I fetched whether these were the same single event or two separate same-day events (one MOI-focused, one Party/Tô Lâm-focused) — flagged as an open gap; if the deliverable cites this, it should say "reported around 1/7/2026" rather than assert a single named conference title with certainty.

### 4. Policy stance on further mergers / connection to Đại hội XIV

- **[A]** As of **9/3/2026** (regular February 2026 government press conference), Phan Trung Tuấn, Director of the Local Government Department at the Ministry of Home Affairs, stated on the record: "Hiện nay Đảng, Nhà nước, Quốc hội, Chính phủ không có chủ trương về việc tiếp tục thực hiện sắp xếp, sáp nhập các tỉnh, thành phố" (there is currently no policy from the Party, State, National Assembly, or Government to continue merging provinces/cities) — explicitly rebutting a circulating rumor of a "second merger to 16 provinces." Source: xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn, fetched — https://xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn/khong-co-chu-truong-tiep-tuc-sap-xep-sap-nhap-cap-tinh-cap-xa-119260306075122013.htm
- **[A]** This "no further province merger" stance is explicitly compatible with continuing **commune-level** consolidation: as reported 1/7/2026 (see §3), the government will keep studying consolidation of the 705 non-conforming communes through 2026 — i.e., no new provincial mergers, but unfinished commune-level tidying continues. Same sources as §3.
- **[B]** Preparation for Đại hội đại biểu toàn quốc lần thứ XIV (the Party's 14th National Congress) is explicitly linked administratively to the provincial merger: newly merged provinces were required to complete draft congress documents and personnel/appointee arrangements for their 2025–2030 provincial Party congresses **before 31/10/2025**, explicitly described in the source as "an important foundation for personnel preparation... for the 14th National Party Congress." This is WebSearch-aggregated from xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn search-summary language, not individually fetched and read in full — capped at B, and the exact deadline (31/10/2025) should be treated as needing one more direct fetch if used as a headline figure. Source (search only): https://xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn/sap-xep-sap-nhap-cap-tinh-cap-xa-hoan-thanh-to-chuc-dai-hoi-dai-bieu-dang-bo-xa-phuong-dac-khu-nhiem-ky-2025-2030-truoc-31-8-2025-119250320143115893.htm (note: this specific URL/title refers to commune-level congresses completing by 31/8/2025; the 31/10/2025 provincial-level deadline came from a different, unfetched search result — treat the two deadlines as distinct and both unverified at A-level).

## Best approaches considered (for the writer)

For a "what actually happened in 12 months" section, the strongest available primary-source anchors are:
1. The **21/4/2026 Quyết định 706/QĐ-TTg** review plan (proves the government itself treats mid-2026 as the formal 1-year checkpoint, with its own internal report deadlines through 20/6/2026) — good for showing due process/institutional self-review, not just PR framing.
2. The **1/7/2026 anniversary reporting** (Deputy PM figures) for the single most citable "what happened in year one" statistics bundle, since it is the government's own retrospective, delivered on the actual anniversary date.
3. The **2/1/2026 six-month progress article** for a mid-point comparison point (useful to show trajectory, not just a single end-of-window snapshot).
4. The **Hoài Đức commune case (10/6/2026)** as the single best-sourced ground-level "problem" anecdote — it names people, a specific commune, and gives an on-record quote, which is much stronger than the generic aggregator claims about IT staffing shortfalls.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- Do **not** collapse the 12/4/2025 Party resolution (60-NQ/TW), the 12/6/2025 NA resolution (202/2025/QH15, passed+effective same day), the 16/6/2025 NA law (72/2025/QH15), and the 1/7/2025 operational go-live into a single date or a single "Nghị quyết 202" — they are four distinct documents/dates at three different tiers (Party / National Assembly x2 / operational reality).
- Do **not** repeat the "247,000 workforce reduction" figure as if it were specific to the 2025 provincial merger — VTV's own fetched text frames it as cumulative "since 2021."
- Do **not** state the "1,069 district tasks / 106 provincial tasks absorbed by communes" figure as verified — it surfaced only in a search-engine synthesis, not in the article I actually opened on that exact topic. Cap at C or drop.
- Do **not** treat the "4,226 surplus offices" figure as a post-1/7/2025 outcome — it is a **pre-transition** Ministry of Home Affairs estimate published 10/5/2025, before the actual handover; the real post-merger disposal is still in progress with a Q2/Q4 2026 completion deadline (HCMC example).
- Be cautious with the "1/7/2026 conference" — I could not fully confirm from what I fetched whether the Deputy-PM-reported event and the Tô-Lâm-framed VTV piece describe one single event or a cluster of same-day events; say "around 1/7/2026" rather than naming one single conference with full confidence.
